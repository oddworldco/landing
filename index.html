About

Odd World Collective started when two friends, Gaby and Rachel, discovered their mutual passion for creating meaningful products at the cross-section of engineering, analytics, and design.

Inspired by the women around them, they created Odd World Collective, with the mission of bringing together products by and for women.

Uger, a Sustainable Menstrual Pad

Uger is designed by and for women in areas with limited access to menstrual products. Partnering with Uger, a women's group in Rajasthan, India, Rachel pitched and sold the first set of pads to a local non profit. These pads are locally sourced, reusable, and manufactured by women in the community they serve.

The Couple's Journey of Conception

Couples who are trying to have a child only have 6 days in a month where conception can take place. Today, more couples than ever are turning to clinical methods of having a child. The non-clinical, DIY approach to tracking fertility ranges from Basal Body Temperature (BBT) thermometers that require tracking difficult charts to high-tech bracelets that automatically collect a woman's temperature in order to predict her fertile window.

The problem with these methods is that they can be innacurate, expensive, and they only factor in 50% of a couples' fertility. Planning for a baby should be an exciting time in a couple's journey, but the difficulty gaining answers from your body causes stress and even anguish. We want to bring the journey of conceiving a child back to earth.

Now, predicting coulpes can track fertility in their sleep - literally. We have worked with couples to design sleepwear that helps couples improve their chances of getting pregnant by tracking and predicting their fertile window during the time where your data is most accurate.

Lady Mafia

www.ladymafia.rocks

We want to change the narrative of women's history. Lady Mafia is a project to catalogue the impacts of women today and throughout history. Some you may have heard of, many that didn’t make into your history books, and all whose contributions to the world as we know it are powerful and maybe invisible; women who are making the world better, who are doing courageous things in the name of human flourishing.

The media has recently focused lots of attention on women, “women’s issues,” women in the work force, the deficit of women in tech fields, and almost glorifying women who have outstanding impact (you know, “for a girl”). The truth is, women have been rocking out for centuries. For one reason or another, they have been largely left out of history books, save for the token Marie Curies of each epoch that somehow make it in as the exception.

Women’s contributions have gone so undocumented and unnoticed that we are now supposed to believe that the days of great accomplishments by women represent a new chapter for humanity. Not so. Women have been the force behind scientific discoveries, the visionaries of art, the pioneers of literature, the first computer programmers, forensic scientists, and leaders of empires. They have been mothers and wives and spouses and friends and activists and even tyrants.

We aren’t documenting “women engineers,” or “female leaders,” or “girls-who-xyz.” We simply intend to create a catalogue of the awesome ways that humans around us - many who happen to be women - inspire us in their everyday actions. Whether subtle or bold, they are fierce. They are agents of change. They are the LADY MAFIA.
